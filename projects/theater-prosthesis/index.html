Studio Instructor: Cristina Pareño
Spring 2019

Theater Precedent: Total Theater

Architect: Walter Gropius (1927), unbuilt
Project collaborator: Michelle Menkiti

Commissioned by playwright Erwin Piscator and designed by Walter Gropius in 1927, the Total Theater attempted to expand the possibilities of theater by creating a variable theater space, where the configuration of stage and seating could be changed according to the needs of the director. A circular section of the seating had an interchangeable section that could either be filled with seating or small stage to create a classic depth perception stage or a proscenium stage. The circular section in the proscenium stage configuration could additionally rotate 180 degrees to create an arena stage, where the small stage was surrounded by the audience from all four sides.

A stop motion film proposed how the total theater would have been used by the spectator and the configurable stages would become the architecture for the spectacle.

Prosthetic Insertion: a total reimagining of theater

I propose a radical reinvention of the circular section of the total theater. In my new design, I take the mechanistic aspect of the rotation as an event to be witnessed. My alteration to the original design is to increase the slope of the seating in the circular section. A new space is created under the now elevated seats. This empty space becomes a second stage for the audience behind the circular section. The second stage is the stage of the inner workings of this “machine” that controls all of the moving elements on the original stage, and also contains the now exposed machinery which moves the circular section.

In this configuration, half the audience members watch the mechanistic movement as the main theatrical event, and the other half of the audience members watch the product of the mechanistic movement. The circular section rotates to reveal the two audience groups to each others, creating dialogue between “front stage” and “back stage".
